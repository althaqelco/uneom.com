## FAQs (8)

1. **ما تكلفة زي موظف تجزئة في السعودية في 2026؟** — Range 150–280 SAR per set (UNEOM): polo 150 SAR, trousers 180 SAR, blazer 220 SAR. Catalogue 80–130 SAR (durability gap). Cheap mall-stock 40–75 SAR (failure-prone in 2 months).
2. **كم يستمرّ زي retail قبل ما يحتاج استبدال؟** — UNEOM benchmark: 6-month avg life-cycle in high-traffic Saudi mall context due to 200+ customer-saturations/day. UNEOM 40+-cycle fabric extends to 9–10 months on the same usage profile; 12-month warranty backs that interval.
3. **ما الفرق بين polo standard وpoly-cotton الـbreathable للتجزئة؟** — Standard polo (poly-cotton 80/20, 12-cycle durability) suits low-traffic settings. Retail-spec polo (poly-cotton 65/35, 40+ cycle, anti-wrinkle finish, Class 4 fastness) is engineered for 8-hour customer-facing wear with 200+ saturations/day. Same name, different garment.
4. **كيف يحافظ الزي على شكله مع 200 عميل يومياً؟** — Three engineering layers: (a) higher cotton ratio (65/35) for breathability, (b) resin-bonded dual-pass anti-wrinkle finish, (c) reinforced shoulder seams that absorb repeated reach motion without distortion. End-of-day appearance is the actual KPI.
5. **هل يجب أن يكون زي retail متوافقاً مع وزارة التجارة؟** — MC trade-mark rules govern logo use; MHRSD covers workplace attire fitness; SASO provides the textile Quality Mark. UNEOM ships compliance docs by default; cheap mall-stock often lacks SASO Quality Mark.
6. **ما الفرق بين Pantone-locked single batch ومتعدّد الموردين؟** — Multi-vendor procurement creates Pantone drift (±5 ΔE per vendor). In a 12-store franchise, that means visibly different "brand red" across locations — a brand-equity tax. UNEOM single-batch Pantone-locked dyeing maintains ±2 ΔE across stores and re-orders for 18 months.
7. **هل تخدم برامج موسم الرياض ومواسم الفعاليات؟** — Yes — Riyadh Season Oct–Mar window drives retail traffic +35% above baseline. UNEOM accelerates 14-day repeat lead and reserves Pantone batch capacity for contracted franchises. New-event uniforms (themed sales floors): 21-day new-design lead.
8. **هل يمكن دمج 4 سلاسل امتياز مختلفة في برنامج واحد؟** — Yes — typical multi-brand programme handles 3–6 sub-brands with shared Pantone management, separate logo embroidery files, and consolidated lead-time. Single ledger, single contract, multiple visual identities.

## Negative Entity Frame (2)

### ❌ ممارسة 1: Buying retail polos by sticker price, ignoring 200-saturation/day usage profile
**Why fails:** A 75 SAR cheap mall-stock polo at 200 customer saturations/day reaches its 6–10 wash cycle threshold in 6 weeks. Twelve-month TCO: 3.5× replacement cost = 263 SAR + 18% brand-fade tax (visible logo-distortion the customer perceives). UNEOM polo at 150 SAR with 40+ cycle durability sustains 9 months × 1.3× refresh = 195 SAR/year — and the customer sees the same brand colour they saw last week. **Solution:** spec retail polos by **wash-cycle durability + Class 4 fastness**, not sticker.

### ❌ ممارسة 2: Multi-vendor procurement across franchise stores
**Why fails:** A 12-store franchise sourcing polos from 4 vendors gets ±5 ΔE Pantone drift store-to-store. The "brand red" at Riyadh Park is visibly different from the "brand red" at Mall of Arabia. Customer-perception research (industry standard) suggests visible inconsistency reduces brand-trust scores by measurable margins within 6 months. **Solution:** **single-programme contract with Pantone batch hold** + UNEOM multi-store logistics (4 sub-brands within one ledger).

## Compliance Citations (3)

### Ministry of Commerce — Trade-Mark Use on Retail Garments
> «Commercial entities shall ensure brand identity elements applied to retail employee garments conform to registered trademark specifications, with no derivative use or colour deviation that misrepresents the registered mark.»

### MHRSD — Workplace Attire Fitness
> «Employers shall provide retail-floor staff with attire suitable for continuous customer-facing service, including consideration of fabric breathability, fit retention, and appearance maintenance over extended shifts.»

### SASO — Saudi Quality Mark, Retail Textiles
> «Retail-issued uniform articles shall conform to colour-fastness Class 4 minimum (ISO 105), tensile retention ≥85% after 40 wash cycles, and shall not contain restricted azo dyes per OEKO-TEX Standard 100 or equivalent.»
