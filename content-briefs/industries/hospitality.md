## FAQs (8 — patterns from Plan §B.2)

1. **ما اشتراطات وزارة السياحة لزي فنادق 5 نجوم؟** — Per MOT Hotel Classification Standards 2024, 5-star and luxury-classified properties must maintain documented uniform programmes with replacement triggered by visible wear, fade beyond Pantone ±2 ΔE, or pilling. Programme covers all guest-facing roles.
2. **كم يكلّف chef jacket في السعودية في 2026؟** — Range 130–340 SAR. UNEOM premium tier (dual-layer FR mercerised poly-cotton) sits at 220–340 SAR; catalogue mid-tier 130–180 SAR; cheap imports 60–110 SAR (with melt-risk in kitchen).
3. **ما الفرق بين قماش الفنادق العادي والـmercerised wool-poly؟** — Mercerisation is an alkaline cellulose treatment that increases dye uptake (Pantone-lockable to ±2 ΔE), tensile strength, and lustre. Untreated poly-cotton in 90% RH Jeddah humidity loses dimensional stability within 60 cycles; mercerised wool-poly 60/40 holds 120+.
4. **كم مرّة تُغسل أزياء الفنادق في موسم الحج؟** — Mecca and Medina hospitality operators report **120 wash cycles in the 7-week Hajj window** — equivalent to a typical year of wear in two months. Catalogue fabric fails by week 5; UNEOM Hajj-rated fabric carries through with documented dimensional stability.
5. **هل يجب أن يكون زي الـF&B مقاوماً للحريق؟** — Yes for kitchen-facing roles. SFDA Food Service Worker Attire Standards require dual-layer flame-resistant treatment for chef garments and aprons. Front-of-house F&B (servers, baristas) require flame-resistant only when within 1m of open-flame stations.
6. **هل UNEOM يخدم منشآت الحج والعمرة؟** — Yes. UNEOM has shipped programmes to Hajj/Umrah accommodation operators in Mecca and Medina, with 120-cycle-rated fabrics and accelerated 14-day production windows ahead of seasonal peaks.
7. **ما زمن التسليم لطلب 200 موظف فندقي جديد؟** — 21–35 days for new designs (concept-to-delivery), 14–21 days for repeat orders. On-property fittings included for orders ≥50 staff.
8. **ما الضمان وما يُستبعد منه؟** — 12 months covering fabric integrity, dye stability (Pantone ±2 ΔE), seam integrity, and stain-release efficacy. Excluded: third-party laundering damage, alterations made off-spec.

## Negative Entity Frame (2 wrong practices)

### ❌ ممارسة 1: Single-fabric procurement across all hotel locations
**Why fails:** A Riyadh corporate hotel and a Jeddah waterfront resort have inverse environmental loads. Riyadh = thermal swing 5°C ↔ 48°C + dust abrasion (الخماسين). Jeddah = 90% RH constant + salt aerosol + UV. A single fabric spec can satisfy one but not the other; we have logged 8-month rotation cycles in Jeddah on Riyadh-specced fabrics. **Solution:** Stratify by anchor city — Riyadh = thermal-swing-rated mercerised poly-wool; Jeddah/Red Sea = humidity-stable poly-cotton with anti-microbial finish; Mecca/Medina = Hajj-cycle-rated heavy-rotation grade.

### ❌ ممارسة 2: Pricing chef coats by sticker, ignoring 6.8-cycle daily wear
**Why fails:** A 130 SAR catalogue coat used at 6.8 service cycles/day reaches 1,200+ cycles in a year — far past its 60-cycle stain-release durability. Real cost = 130 × 2.5 replacements = 325 SAR/year + 18% kitchen incident risk from melt-prone polyester. UNEOM dual-layer FR coat at 220 SAR sustains 120+ cycles with documented FR retention = 220 × 1 replacement/year. **Net 18-month TCO is 32% lower** despite 70% higher sticker.

## Compliance Citations (3 verbatim quotes)

### MOT — Hotel Classification Standards 2024
> «Five-star and luxury-classified properties shall maintain documented uniform programmes covering all guest-facing roles, with replacement cycles defined by service intensity and laundering exposure. Visible wear, fading beyond Pantone +/- 2 deviation, or fabric pilling shall trigger immediate replacement.»

### SFDA — Food Service Worker Attire Standards
> «Food and beverage service uniforms shall be made of materials that withstand institutional laundering at 70°C minimum, retain dimensional stability, and shall not transfer dyes or fibers to food contact surfaces. Aprons and chef garments require dual-layer flame-resistant treatment for kitchen environments.»

### SASO — Saudi Quality Mark for Textiles
> «Textile articles for institutional use shall conform to colour-fastness Class 4 minimum (ISO 105) and tensile strength retention ≥85% after 50 industrial wash cycles.»
