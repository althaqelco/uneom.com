## FAQs (8)

1. **ما اشتراطات HCIS لزي شركات الأمن في 2025؟** — HCIS 2025 update specifies: tactical garments shall be ripstop construction ≥220 gsm, reinforced load-bearing zones, moisture-wicking inner layer, ANSI/HCIS-aligned high-visibility for outdoor patrol, and ballistic carriers rated NIJ Level IIIA minimum for armed contexts. UNEOM tendering observation: 73% of Saudi firms still operate non-compliant.
2. **ما الفرق بين «ballistic-rated» التسويقي و«NIJ-certified» الحقيقي؟** — *Ballistic-rated* is a marketing label that means nothing without lab data. *NIJ-certified* is the US National Institute of Justice classification (Level IIA, II, IIIA, III, IV) — internationally recognised, lab-tested per Standard-0101.06. UNEOM ballistic carriers ship with NIJ test reports per batch.
3. **كم يكلّف زي حارس أمن كامل في السعودية في 2026؟** — Duty set 280–520 SAR (UNEOM HCIS-compliant). Add 1,400–2,800 SAR for NIJ IIIA ballistic carrier where required. Imported generic 180–280 SAR (HCIS-compliance gap risk).
4. **هل يجوز استيراد زي أمن من خارج السعودية؟** — Yes, but the imported article must independently demonstrate HCIS 2025 conformity + SASO Quality Mark for textile. The audit failure rate among imported tactical uniforms in 2024 was high — UNEOM ships with full Saudi-compliance documentation by default.
5. **ما اشتراطات MHRSD للساعات الطويلة لزي الأمن؟** — MHRSD Workplace Hours regulate 12-hour shift rotations; the implicit attire requirement is fabric and construction that does not degrade under continuous wear. UNEOM long-shift fabrics are lab-tested for moisture-wicking and thermal stability across 30°C overnight swings.
6. **هل UNEOM يخدم فعاليات Riyadh Season وExpo 2030؟** — Yes — event-scale programmes with on-site fittings, multi-tier role grading, and accelerated 18-day repeat lead. Expo 2030 + Riyadh Season scale-up (~40,000 incremental security personnel) is within UNEOM operational capacity.
7. **ما الـcomfort engineering في زي الأمن لورديّة 12 ساعة؟** — Three layers of engineering: (a) ripstop poly-cotton outer (240 gsm) for abrasion resistance, (b) poly-mesh inner moisture-wicking (35% improvement vs cotton), (c) reinforced load-bearing zones at shoulder/elbow/knee that distribute carrier weight without pressure points.
8. **ما الضمان وما يُستثنى؟** — 18 months covering structural integrity, reinforcement panel adhesion, and inner-layer moisture-wicking retention. Excluded: ballistic-impact damage (separate carrier replacement protocol), modification damage, third-party laundering deviation.

## Negative Entity Frame (2)

### ❌ ممارسة 1: Buying "ballistic-rated" vests without NIJ certificate
**Why fails:** "Ballistic-rated" is unregulated; "NIJ-certified" is. A 2024 audit of an imported "ballistic" vest used by a Saudi facility security team revealed a foam-core insert with no measurable resistance to .357 SIG (NIJ IIIA threat). The cost gap (1,800 SAR imported vs 2,400 SAR NIJ-IIIA) is dwarfed by the liability gap. **Solution:** require **NIJ Standard-0101.06 lab certificate per batch** + HCIS-aligned carrier construction. UNEOM ships per-batch reports.

### ❌ ممارسة 2: Single-spec uniforms across indoor + outdoor + event posts
**Why fails:** A facility security guard at an indoor Riyadh diplomatic post faces 21°C climate-controlled environment; the same uniform on outdoor patrol at a Dammam petrochemical perimeter faces 48°C summer days and 8°C winter overnight (حرّ المراقبة swing). Single-spec polyester duty wear suffocates indoor and freezes outdoor. **Solution:** stratify by deployment — indoor poly-cotton 65/35 standard; outdoor reinforced ripstop 240 gsm with thermal layering option; event-ops with high-mobility moisture-wicking emphasis.

## Compliance Citations (3)

### HCIS — Industrial Security Personnel Uniform Standard 2025
> «Security operatives at HCIS-licensed industrial sites shall wear documented uniform programmes meeting ripstop construction ≥220 gsm, reinforced load-bearing zones, integrated high-visibility for outdoor deployment, and ballistic carrier conformity to NIJ classification levels matched to threat assessment.»

### MHRSD — Workplace Hours and Attire
> «Employers operating shift rotations exceeding 8 hours shall ensure attire fitness for continuous wear, including thermal regulation appropriate to deployment environment and moisture management.»

### NIJ Standard-0101.06 — Ballistic Resistance of Body Armor
> «Body armor shall be classified by tested resistance to specified projectile threats; Level IIIA shall protect against .357 SIG and .44 Magnum FMJ-FN at specified velocities.»
