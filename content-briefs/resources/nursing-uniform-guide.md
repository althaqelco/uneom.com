### 6. The 12-Hour Shift Engineering Block (~400 words)
What goes into a uniform that survives a 12-hour Saudi tertiary hospital shift:
- Antimicrobial efficacy through 80+ industrial wash cycles
- 4-direction stretch to accommodate clinical movement
- Reinforced stress seams at shoulder + crotch (where uniform fails first)
- Moisture-wicking inner-layer for ER + ICU heat-stress contexts
- Pantone-locked across re-orders for visual consistency in shift handoffs

### 8. Decision Framework (~220 words)
"Which UNEOM scrub for which nursing role?":
- ER + ICU → Premium Scrub Set
- OR (under sterile gown) → Premium Scrub Set
- Ward + Outpatient → Classic Scrub Set
- Pediatric ward → Classic + Class 2 OEKO-TEX
- Female nurses → Hijab-integrated variant of any of the above

### 9. Common Mistakes (~250 words — 4 anti-patterns)

1. **Cotton-100% scrubs in 12-hour ICU rotation** — antimicrobial efficacy gone at 30 cycles, fabric stress-seam failure at 40 cycles
2. **Hijab "patch" retrofit** — non-integrated retrofits fail Pantone, fit, and SFDA-registration; integrated variants are standard
3. **Scrub-only spec ignoring stress-seam reinforcement** — uniform fails at the shoulder before the fabric — engineering at the seam matters more than GSM alone
4. **No per-batch AATCC documentation** — antimicrobial claim without lab attribution is marketing not clinical defense
