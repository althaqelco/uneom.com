## Required Sections (B.6)

### 1. Executive Summary (~180 words, Speakable)
- **Sustainability is duration first** — a 14-month rotation produces 43% less waste than 8-month
- 3 pillars: Chemical safety (OEKO-TEX) + Recycled material (30–50% post-consumer) + Closed-loop take-back
- Anti-greenwashing test: certificate per batch, lab-attributed numbers, end-of-life accountability
- Vision 2030 + Saudi Green Initiative alignment as procurement baseline
- 8–12% pricing premium typically offset by replacement-cycle extension within year 1

### 5. The Three Sustainability Pillars (~600 words across 3 H2 subsections — ~200 each)

- **5.1 Chemical Safety (OEKO-TEX 100)** — fiber + dye + finish chemical thresholds; Class 2 vs Class 3 distinction; per-batch lab certification methodology
- **5.2 Recycled Material Sourcing** — 30–50% post-consumer recycled polyester; GRS chain-of-custody; fiber families that support recycled variants (hospitality, retail) vs those that don't (HRC2 FR)
- **5.3 End-of-Life Take-Back** — annual collection cadence; sortation (alteration-rebadge for re-issue + recycling-stream for end-of-life); industrial-wipe transformation methodology; closed-loop accounting

### 6. Anti-Greenwashing Block (~350 words — the core counter-narrative)
Six tests for genuine sustainability claims:
1. Per-batch certificate (not "OEKO-TEX certified family" without batch attribution)
2. Lab-attributed numbers (not "eco-friendly" marketing)
3. Material composition disclosure (recycled % verifiable)
4. End-of-life accountability (take-back contracted, not aspirational)
5. Duration validation (rotation cycle commitment, not feel-good intent)
6. Vision 2030 / Saudi Green Initiative alignment documented

The 43% waste reduction math: 8-month catalogue rotation = 1.5× replacements/year × workforce; 14-month engineered rotation = 0.86× replacements/year. Per 1,000-employee programme: 640 fewer garment retirements/year.

### 7. Comparison Matrix — Sustainability Tiers (Markdown)

| Dimension | UNEOM OEKO-TEX | UNEOM Recycled | UNEOM Closed-Loop | Generic "eco" claim |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Chemical safety cert | OEKO-TEX 100 per batch | OEKO-TEX 100 per batch | OEKO-TEX 100 per batch | Marketing claim |
| Recycled content | None | 30–50% PCR | 30–50% PCR | Often unverified |
| Take-back programme | No | No | Annual collection + recycling | None |
| Reporting for ESG | Per-batch cert pack | + GRS chain-of-custody | + closed-loop documentation | None |
| Pricing premium | 0–3% | 5–8% | 8–12% | Variable, often inflated |
| Year-1 TCO impact | Neutral (or saving via duration) | Neutral | -3 to -8% (rotation extension) | Often +TCO with no ESG defensible benefit |

### 8. Decision Framework (~250 words)
"Which sustainability tier?":
- Regulated procurement → OEKO-TEX baseline mandatory
- ESG reporting commitment → Closed-Loop tier
- Cost-conscious + sustainability-curious → Recycled tier
- Hospital + Education + Hospitality → OEKO-TEX standard
- Manufacturing FR → OEKO-TEX (FR fabric not recyclable; closed-loop recycle into industrial wipes)

### 9. Common Mistakes (~280 words — 4 anti-patterns)

1. **"Sustainable" without per-batch certificate** — family-level claims wash out batch-by-batch
2. **Recycled content without GRS chain-of-custody** — "30% recycled" without verification is unprovable
3. **Aspirational take-back without contract** — collected good intentions, not collected garments
4. **Material focus to exclusion of duration** — a 6-month-rotation "sustainable" garment is environmentally worse than an 18-month engineered garment
