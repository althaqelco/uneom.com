### 6. Comparison Matrix — Procurement Models (Markdown)

| Dimension | Annual Contract (UNEOM) | Quarterly Spot Buying | Multi-Vendor Catalogue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing predictability | Locked annual rate | Q4 +20% premium risk | Variable per vendor |
| Pantone consistency | ±2 ΔE single batch | Drift each spot-buy | ±5 ΔE drift across vendors |
| Lead time | 14–21 days repeat | 30–45 days spot | 30–60 days |
| Compliance audit pack | Standard (Documented tier) | Per-order add-on | Inconsistent |
| TCO 18-month per employee | Baseline | +12–18% above baseline | +18–25% above baseline |
| Vendor management overhead | Single contact | Spot vendor scramble | 4–7 contracts to manage |

### 7. RFP Template Block (~300 words)
The 12 RFP must-haves:
- Compliance scope (industry regulators)
- Pantone reference + drift tolerance
- Wash-cycle validation requirement
- Replacement-cycle SLA
- On-site fitting commitment
- Joiner-kit dispatch SLA
- Pricing tiers + volume breaks
- Warranty scope + duration
- Audit pack inclusion
- Sustainability tier (OEKO-TEX / Recycled / Closed-Loop)
- Manufacturer vs distributor declaration
- Contract term + renewal mechanics

### 8. Decision Framework (~250 words)
"Annual Contract or Spot?" decision tree:
- Headcount stable >100 → Annual
- High-turnover roles → Annual + Programme Management
- Rapidly scaling (NEOM/RSP context) → Annual with quarterly headcount adjustment clause
- Non-strategic uniform need → Spot acceptable
- Brand-critical → Annual mandatory

### 9. Common Mistakes (~280 words — 4 anti-patterns)

1. **Comparing line-item price without TCO lens** — 130 SAR cheap vs 180 SAR engineered: cheap costs 360 SAR/year (2× replacement) vs 180 SAR engineered (1×). Sticker is the wrong metric.
2. **No Pantone batch hold in contract** — every re-order without batch hold drifts ±5 ΔE. After 3 drift cycles, brand identity is visibly compromised.
3. **Spot-buying through Q4** — موسم الميزانيات (budget season) drives 75% of orders into 8 weeks; suppliers extract 20% premium; lead times stretch to 60 days.
4. **Distributor middle layer** — 15–25% markup with no design + production + warranty integration. Every intermediary layer = lost engineering coordination.
