## Negative Entity Frame (1 — city-specific)

### ❌ ممارسة شائعة في الرياض: Single-spec uniform across the seasonal swing
**Why fails:** A summer-spec poly-cotton 65/35 (200 gsm) at 43°C+ in July is the right call — and the same fabric at 5°C overnight in January (الأربعينية) fails as thermal protection. We have logged Riyadh corporate accounts that contracted single-spec programmes in May, then absorbed an unplanned 35% mid-winter spend on coats and layered alternatives. **Solution:** UNEOM Riyadh Programme ships **summer set + transitional set + Arba'eeniyah thermal layer** within one annual contract — Pantone-locked across all three so brand identity is preserved year-round.

## Local Case Study Snippet (~150 words)

> A Riyadh tertiary referral hospital (12-hospital portfolio, 4,200 clinical staff) had been procuring scrubs from four separate vendors over 6 years. Pantone drift across vendors created visible variation between wards. AATCC-100 antimicrobial efficacy claims were unverified on three of the four contracts. The 18-month total programme spend reached 11.4M SAR with documented infection-control concerns from the IPC team during a 2024 internal audit. UNEOM consolidated the four vendors into a single Pantone-locked programme: silver-ion bonded poly-cotton 65/35, AATCC-100 documented per batch, 18-month warranty. Deliverables: 4,200 scrubs over 8 weeks, on-site fittings across 12 sites, 18-month TCO −22% (8.9M SAR). Single ward, single batch, single colour — and a Pantone batch hold for joiners and replacements through 2027.
