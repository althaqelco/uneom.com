2. **هل يحتاج العمل في تبوك إلى زي شتوي؟** — Yes during Shita' al-'Ashara (the ten coldest days of January) and surrounding weeks: highland January overnight lows reach −3°C; midday is mild but morning shifts and overnight perimeter security require thermal protection. UNEOM Tabuk-spec deploys removable thermal liners that snap into the standard programme garment.

4. **هل يخدم UNEOM موقع مشروع البحر الأحمر ونيوم؟** — Yes — both project zones are within Tabuk programme service area. Construction-site perimeter security operators, project administration offices, and emerging early-phase hospitality (project accommodation) are all served via the Tabuk programme with 48-hour delivery from Jeddah depot.
5. **ما زمن التسليم في تبوك ومنطقة نيوم؟** — Joiner kits 48 hours from Jeddah depot. Repeat orders 14–21 days. New programme launches 28–45 days. Programme contracts above 200 staff (typical for NEOM/RSP construction phases) receive same-day urgent dispatch protocols and dedicated logistics manager.

## Negative Entity Frame (1)

### ❌ Buying Riyadh-spec uniforms for Tabuk-region operations
**Why fails:** Riyadh and Jeddah specifications assume daily operating temperatures within a 35–48°C envelope. Tabuk's 50°C annual swing — particularly the −3°C Shita' al-'Ashara overnight lows in surrounding highlands — falls outside that envelope on the cold side. We have logged construction-site security operators in Tabuk that deployed Riyadh-spec lightweight performance polyester through the winter; thermal-discomfort complaints, productivity loss, and ad-hoc layering with personal cold-weather garments degraded the uniformed presentation requirement. **Solution:** UNEOM Tabuk Programme = **city-specific layered thermal regulation as a single integrated garment system** — not a Riyadh garment with extra coats. The integrated specification is the engineering answer; ad-hoc layering is the failure mode.

## Local Case Study Snippet (~150 words)

> A NEOM construction-zone perimeter security operator (240 personnel, 12-hour rotational shifts including overnight perimeter patrol) had been issued Riyadh-spec lightweight performance uniforms across the contract's first six months. By December 2024, thermal-discomfort complaints accumulated across morning and overnight shifts; supervisors reported personnel layering personal jackets over the issued uniform — degrading the uniformed presentation that the project's security contract required. UNEOM was engaged for the Q1 2025 issue: Tabuk-spec layered thermal-regulation uniform with detachable inner liner, wind-resistant outer shell, and reinforced load-bearing zones for vest-mounted patrol equipment. Outcome: thermal-discomfort complaints dropped to near-zero through Shita' al-'Ashara January 2025; overnight-shift productivity tracked unchanged through the cold weeks; uniformed presentation maintained without ad-hoc layering. The operator extended the programme to 480 personnel for Q3 2025 as the construction phase ramped.
