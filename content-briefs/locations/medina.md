## Negative Entity Frame (1)

### ❌ Applying Mecca Hajj-grade specifications to Medina year-round operations
**Why fails:** Mecca-grade fabrics are over-engineered for Medina's continuous-flow conditions — they cost ~22% more per garment without delivering operational advantage in steady-state laundering. Medina operators contracting Mecca-grade specifications pay a premium for compression resistance they will never need; they should pay for highland-climate optimisation instead. **Solution:** UNEOM ships distinct city-specific programmes — Mecca = Hajj-grade compression resistance; Medina = highland-stable premium mercerised. Same Pantone library, different engineering envelope.

## Local Case Study Snippet (~150 words)

> A Prophet's Mosque-adjacent hotel (640-uniform fleet, four guest-facing roles) had been on a catalogue hospitality programme with 11-month rotation cycles for four consecutive years. Pantone drift across re-orders created visible role-to-role variation; humidity-driven fade was negligible (Medina's stability is forgiving) but laundering volume drove cumulative pilling that triggered earlier-than-needed rotations. UNEOM proposed a Medina-spec programme: mercerised wool-poly 60/40 at 220 gsm with full reactive-dye chemistry, brass fasteners, fluorocarbon-free C0 stain-release. Pantone batch held in Medina depot for joiners and replacements. Outcome: rotation extended from 11 to 16 months without compromising appearance standards; 18-month TCO −18%; Pantone consistency held across two re-order cycles. The hotel's housekeeping team noted measurable reduction in mid-rotation replacement requests — evidence that a year-round-engineered fabric outperforms a stress-rated Mecca-grade applied where it isn't needed.
