## Negative Entity Frame (1)

### ❌ Buying Riyadh-spec uniforms for Jeddah operations
**Why fails:** Riyadh's textile spec (poly-cotton 65/35, untreated reactive dye, standard zinc fasteners) is engineered for dry heat — not 90% RH coastal exposure. Hotels and corporate offices in Jeddah that procured Riyadh-spec catalogue uniforms log measurable fade by month 3, fastener corrosion by month 2, and fabric pilling by month 4. The catalogue saving is 18% — and the rotation acceleration is 50% (8-month cycles vs 14–16). **Solution:** UNEOM Jeddah Programme ships **mercerised wool-poly + hydrolysis-resistant reactive dye + corrosion-resistant fasteners** as a Jeddah-specific spec, distinct from the Riyadh-region default.

## Local Case Study Snippet (~150 words)

> A waterfront resort on Jeddah Corniche (210 staff, four guest-facing roles) had been procuring catalogue hospitality uniforms with 8-month rotation cycles for three consecutive years. Pantone drift across re-orders created visible variation between front-desk and concierge roles within six months of each cycle; humidity-driven fade pushed photographic re-shoots of marketing material twice annually. UNEOM consolidated into a single Pantone-locked Jeddah programme: mercerised wool-poly 60/40 with hydrolysis-resistant reactive dye, brass fasteners, fluorocarbon-free C0 stain-release. Programme spec rated for 18-month accelerated-humidity exposure. Outcome: rotation extended from 8 months to 16; Pantone drift held within ±2 ΔE; year-1 TCO −24% on the same role headcount; a single Pantone batch held in Jeddah depot for joiners and replacements through Q4 2027.
