## Negative Entity Frame (1)

### ❌ Applying Riyadh or Jeddah specifications to Abha mountain operations
**Why fails:** Riyadh-spec lightweight performance polyester is engineered for 43°C dry heat — it suffocates in Abha's 60–75% mountain-mist humidity year-round. Jeddah-spec mercerised wool-poly assumes 90% RH coastal humidity — it over-engineers Abha's intermediate envelope while missing the elevation-driven UV intensity entirely. The result on either spec is uniform fabric that pills early and fades unevenly. **Solution:** UNEOM Abha Programme = **water-repellent C0 outer + thermal-blend inner + UV-rated reactive dye**, deployed as a city-specific specification distinct from inland and coastal programmes. The 2,200m elevation is a textile-engineering condition, not a marketing detail.

## Local Case Study Snippet (~150 words)

> An Asir-region eco-tourism camp operator (90 staff across guest-services and grounds-maintenance roles, expanding through 2026) had been on a generic "outdoor performance" uniform programme procured through a Riyadh distributor. Within 5 months of operations, the staff uniforms showed two failure modes: humidity-driven pilling on the polyester polos under year-round mist exposure, and dye fade on the cotton trouser fabric from elevation-driven UV. Mid-rotation replacement was unfunded; visible inconsistency between new and aged uniforms was visible to guests. UNEOM proposed an Abha-specific programme: water-repellent C0 outer treatment, thermal-blend mid-layer for cold-weather months, UV-rated reactive dyes tested for 2,200m elevation. Pantone batch held in Jeddah depot for 72-hour delivery. Outcome: 12-month rotation cycle achieved at first attempt; UV fade controlled to ±2 ΔE; humidity-driven pilling not observed; 18-month TCO −24%. The operator extended the programme to its second-phase camp expansion in Q4 2025.
