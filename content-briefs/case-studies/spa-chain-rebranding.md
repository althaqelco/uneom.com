## Required Sections (B.7)

### 1. Hero Before/After Slider
Compare old uniform + signage → new uniform + signage as the unified visual rebrand.

### 2. Challenge Block (~200 words)
8-location wellness spa chain in transition: aging brand identity, declining brand-recognition surveys, marketing team planning a 12-week rebrand. Traditional approach: design new logo + collateral first, uniforms last (often 6+ months later). Risk: 6-month visible inconsistency between rebranded signage and old uniforms eroding brand-recognition gains. UNEOM proposed inverse sequence — uniform-first.

### 3. Approach Block (~250 words)
Sequence inversion:
- **Week 1–4:** UNEOM Custom Design + Bulk Ordering for new uniform programme (mercerised wool-poly + hijab variant, Pantone-locked single batch, role-graded across reception + therapist + front-of-house)
- **Week 5–8:** Uniform programme deployed to all 8 locations simultaneously (joiner-kit logistics through Jeddah depot)
- **Week 9–12:** Signage + collateral rollout aligned with already-deployed uniform Pantone

### 4. Solution Detail (~250 words)
Pantone-locked across 8 locations. Hijab-coordinated standard. Replacement-cycle SLA included. Single-batch fabric ensures Year-2 re-orders match Year-1 deployment.

### 5. Numbers Block
- **8 locations** rebranded
- **12 weeks** total rollout (uniform week 4 deployment + signage week 8–12)
- **Uniform-first sequence** validated as faster brand-recognition vehicle
- **Brand-recognition survey lift** documented at 6 months (figure pending real-data confirmation)

### 6. Quote (only if consent)

### 7. Lessons Learned
1. Uniform-first rebrand is faster to market than collateral-first
2. Pantone-lock across 8 locations is the engineering enabler, not an afterthought
3. Hijab-coordinated variants must be in scope from day-1 design
