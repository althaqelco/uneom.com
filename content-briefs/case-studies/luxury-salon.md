### 2. Challenge Block (~200 words)
6-city luxury salon chain with 120 staff, vendor base of 4+ catalogue suppliers per city. Pantone drift between Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, Medina, Mecca, Khobar locations created visible inconsistency for clients travelling between branches. No hijab-coordinated variant — staff added personal accessories that broke the brand-coordinated visual language. No replacement-cycle planning — staff turnover (~25% annually) led to ad-hoc spot procurement at 20% premium.

### 3. Approach Block (~250 words)
- Single Pantone batch held for 18 months minimum
- Hijab-coordinated variants standard across all 6 cities (Pantone-matched)
- Programme contract with city-specific joiner kit SLAs (24h Riyadh + Jeddah + Dammam, 48h others)
- Quarterly programme review across all 6 locations
- 14-day repeat lead from city-nearest depot (Riyadh + Jeddah + Dammam Metro)

### 4. Solution Detail (~250 words)
Mercerised wool-poly tropical with brand-coordinated dual variant: standard + hijab-integrated. Chemical-resistance treatment for salon-product exposure (perming, colouring, treatment chemicals). Reinforced apron pockets for tools.

### 5. Numbers Block
- **120 staff** across 6 cities unified
- **18-month Pantone batch hold** for cross-city consistency
- **24-hour to 48-hour joiner-kit SLA** depending on city
- **±2 ΔE Pantone consistency** across 6 cities
- **14-day repeat lead** post-launch

### 6. Quote (only if consent)

### 7. Lessons Learned
1. Multi-city Pantone consistency is the brand-equity dimension that erodes silently — clients notice between branches
2. Hijab-coordinated variants are baseline, not premium — non-coordinated retrofits visibly fail
3. Quarterly programme review across cities catches drift before it becomes visible
