## Required Sections (B.7)

### 1. Hero Before/After Slider
Before: 6 different uniform programmes from 6 different vendors. After: 1 UNEOM programme managing 6 distinct Pantone identities in single contract.

### 2. Challenge Block (~200 words)
Beauty retail group with 6 sub-brands (high-end perfumes, mass-market cosmetics, premium skincare, mid-tier makeup, professional artistry, gift retail) operating 24 stores across Saudi Arabia. Each sub-brand had distinct Pantone identity intentionally — corporate strategy required visual differentiation. But 6 procurement contracts, 6 lead-time variances, 6 audit overheads. Group HQ wanted procurement consolidation without sacrificing sub-brand visual differentiation.

### 3. Approach Block (~250 words)
UNEOM scoped a multi-brand programme:
- **Single contract** with the group HQ
- **6 distinct Pantone batches** held under single programme (one per sub-brand)
- **Shared logistics** (single dispatch network across 24 stores)
- **Role-graded set per sub-brand** (high-end vs mass-market vs professional differs in fabric tier)
- **Single ledger + audit pack** for procurement + compliance
- **Quarterly programme review** with all 6 sub-brand operations directors

### 4. Solution Detail (~250 words)
- **Fabric tier per sub-brand:** premium = cotton-spandex twill with metallic-thread; mass = standard cotton-spandex; professional = ripstop chemical-resistant
- **Embroidery tier per sub-brand:** metallic-thread for high-end; twin-needle standard for mid; tactical-grade for professional
- **Pantone management:** 6 distinct batches, all held 18 months minimum, all twin-needle Pantone-locked
- **Single dispatch network:** Riyadh + Jeddah + Dammam Metro depots

### 5. Numbers Block (Speakable)
- **6 sub-brands** unified under 1 contract
- **24 stores** across Saudi Arabia
- **6 Pantone batches** managed within single programme
- **3 fabric tiers** (premium / mass / professional)
- **18-month contract pricing lock**
- **1 ledger + 1 audit pack** for procurement consolidation

### 6. Quote from Client
Group COO with title (consent required). Otherwise omit.

### 7. Lessons Learned
1. **Multi-brand programme consolidation ≠ uniform homogenisation** — 6 distinct Pantone identities preserved within single contract
2. **Per-sub-brand fabric tier** matters — premium and mass-market can't share fabric envelope without one of them feeling wrong
3. **Single ledger transforms procurement economics** — 6 contracts → 1 contract eliminates lead-time variance and audit overhead
