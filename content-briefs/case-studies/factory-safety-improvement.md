## Required Sections (B.7)

### 1. Hero Before/After Slider
HRC1 synthetic FR (degraded) → HRC2 inherent FR cotton (intact at 18 months).

### 2. Challenge Block (~200 words)
Petrochemical site in Al-Jubail experienced a near-miss arc-flash incident in Q4 2024. Site safety lead reviewed FR coverall performance data and discovered synthetic FR treatment degrading faster than expected in 50°C plant-floor heat-soak: ATPV retention falling below NFPA 70E threshold by month 11, far short of the 18-month claimed envelope. HCIS quarterly audit was 14 weeks away.

### 3. Approach Block (~250 words)
UNEOM scoped a 14-week full-programme migration:
- **Week 1–2:** Per-worker measurement (450 frontline staff) on-site fittings
- **Week 3–8:** Production block of 450 HRC2 inherent FR cotton 88/12 nylon coveralls + ANSI 107 Class 2 hi-vis integration + chloride-resistant brass fasteners (Eastern coastal-grade)
- **Week 9–11:** Per-garment serialisation + UL ATPV report attached to each unit
- **Week 12–13:** Phased deployment with shift-coverage guarantee (no operational gap during transition)
- **Week 14:** HCIS audit prep + audit pack assembly

### 4. Solution Detail (~250 words)
- **Fabric:** Inherent FR cotton 88/12 nylon at 320 GSM (vs synthetic FR 250 GSM)
- **ATPV rating:** ≥8 cal/cm² UL-tested per batch (vs 4–6 cal/cm² synthetic)
- **Hardware:** Brass fasteners (Eastern coastal chloride zone)
- **Per-garment serial:** Imprinted on care label, mapped to UL test report + production batch
- **Audit pack:** Per-batch UL ATPV reports, HCIS 2024 conformity declaration, ANSI 107 Class 2 conformity, chloride-resistance documentation

### 5. Numbers Block (Speakable)
- **450 frontline workers** migrated in 14 weeks
- **HCIS audit passed first review** with zero non-conformities
- **24 months mean coverall lifespan** in 50°C plant-floor heat-soak
- **0 garment-performance incidents** in 18-month operating window
- **Audit prep time:** 2 days → 30 minutes via per-garment serialisation

### 6. Quote from Client
Site safety officer with title (with consent). **Critical for this case study — operational credibility hinges on named source.** Otherwise omit section.

### 7. Lessons Learned (3 takeaways)
1. **Synthetic FR ≠ inherent FR** in operational reality — lab numbers diverge under heat-soak; field-validated migration is the only proof
2. **Per-garment serialisation transforms HCIS audit economics** — 2 days → 30 minutes; the safety lead's audit overhead drops; HCIS confidence rises
3. **Phased deployment with shift-coverage guarantee** is the migration enabler — full-stop replacement isn't operationally viable in 24/7 industrial sites
