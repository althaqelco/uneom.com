## Required Sections (B.7)

### 1. Hero Before/After Slider
Wet zone before/after, treatment zone, relaxation zone — three sliders side-by-side or rotating.

### 2. Challenge Block (~200 words)
Five-star hotel spa with 60 staff, 3 functional zones, mixed catalogue procurement. Wet-zone uniforms (showering between treatments, water exposure throughout shift) failing within 4 months — antimicrobial efficacy gone, fabric breakdown at high-cycle washing temperatures (90°C+). Treatment-zone uniforms (massage therapists, facialists) needed quiet drape + breathable comfort — catalogue polyester was loud and uncomfortable. Relaxation-zone hosts and reception lacked the wool-poly tropical drape that signals five-star presence.

### 3. Approach Block (~250 words)
UNEOM scoped a zone-graded uniform programme:
- **Wet zone (15 staff):** cotton-spandex twill 200 GSM with antimicrobial bonded silver-ion + Teflon stain-shield, 100+ wash cycle validated at 90°C
- **Treatment zone (30 staff):** breathable mercerised cotton-poly with quiet drape + role-specific apron pockets for treatment tools
- **Relaxation zone (15 staff):** wool-poly tropical mercerised half-canvas with brand-coordinated silk scarf
- All Pantone-locked single batch for cohesive guest presentation across zones

### 4. Solution Detail Block (~250 words)
Programme contract with on-site fittings, joiner-kit SLA, replacement-cycle planning. MOT Hotel Classification audit pack delivered with shipment. 14-day repeat lead post-launch.

### 5. Numbers Block
- **60 staff** uniformed across 3 zones
- **3 zone-graded fabric specifications** unified under single Pantone
- **8 weeks** concept-to-delivery
- **Pantone ±2 ΔE** brand consistency
- **Wet-zone replacement cycle** extended from 4 months to 12 months

### 6. Quote from Client
Hotel GM with title + property name (only if consent secured). Otherwise omit section.

### 7. Lessons Learned (3 takeaways)
1. **Zone-grading is mandatory in spa contexts** — wet, treatment, and relaxation have different physical envelopes; one-fabric-fits-all fails one of them
2. **Pantone-lock cohesion ≠ fabric uniformity** — same colour family, different fabrics is the engineering answer
3. **MOT audit pack is procurement table-stakes** — not delivering it makes the GM's compliance review harder
